Chile Con Queso

Description

A big bowl of gold

If you're a University of Oklahoma alumnus you've probably sat down at The Mont for a drink and a generous helping of the restaurant's famous four-cheese queso dip. The restaurant is currently owned by three alumni and the dip (which inspired this recipe) is loaded with pico de gallo and chunks of browned sausage. It's the perfect snack for tip-off or half-time.

Ingredients

  • One 1-pound package refrigerated hot breakfast sausage
  • 1 onion, diced
  • One 2-pound block deli American cheese
  • One 10-ounce can diced tomatoes and green chiles
  • 2 cans chopped green chiles
  • 1 jalapeno, diced
  • Chips, for serving

Steps

  1. Brown the sausage and onion in a large nonstick skillet, breaking the sausage up as you go. Once browned, drain off some of the fat.
  2. Chop the cheese block into cubes and add along with the diced tomatoes and green chiles and chopped green chiles, juice and all.
  3. Cook over a low heat until it all starts coming together. Then stir in the diced jalapeno.
  4. Serve with chips.
